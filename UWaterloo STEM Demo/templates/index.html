About This Project

The Facts

In Canada, the wage gap between men and women widen by 14% as they progress throughout their careers

Women in STEM earn ~19.5% less than men

Women are not staying in STEM careers. More than 20% of engineering graduates are women, but only 11% are practicing engineers.

Accenture's Social Innovation

Accenture partnered with a leading Canadian university to support their commitment to HeForShe's IMPACT 10X10X10, a global campaign aimed at encouraging institutions and leaders to remove the social and cultural barriers that prevent women and girls from achieving their potential. As part of this initiative, the university committed to boosting female participation in STEM experiences to build future women leaders and enhance female faculty,

Data Exploration

Understanding trends in student data, in a dataset of over 20,000 students. Looked at data points around demographics, academics, admissions, registration, and co-op records.

Unsupervised Machine Learning

Grouping students into 6 different profiles through 96 iterations of the clustering algorithm to understand prominent success factors and the strength of correlations between factors.

Human Centric Recommendations

Provided insights on how to better attract, empower, and support women in STEM in academics and co-ops. Insights also be leveraged to enhance current academic and experiential learning programs, to further promote student success.
