HOW DID ACCENTURE HELP?

A pro-bono team from Accenture analyzed internal and external data sets ranging from academic records to co-op evaluations to discover trends and cluster student, using machine learning. These clusters were used to create 6 distinct "personas" - each with different strengths, weaknesses, and propensity to major in STEM. Design thinking workshops were then conducted to identify different initiatives the university could invest in, to encourage more incoming and existing female students to pursue educational and employment opportunties in STEM

1. External Research
We have conducted research of various external sources to guide our analysis and provide deeper context to barriers experienced by women in STEM
2. Exploratory Analysis
We conducted an exploratory analysis on the data provided by IAP to develop preliminary insights and to prioritize features to be included and engineered in the clustering model.
3. Developing a Model
Data was cleansed to remove outliers, adjust for inflation and multicollinearity, and replace categorical values with numerical values that can be run through a statistical model. New variables were engineered and a k-means clustering algorithm was used to groups students into similar "clusters."
