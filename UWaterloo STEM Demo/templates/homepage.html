PROMOTING STUDENT SUCCESS WITH DATA DRIVEN GUIDANCE

The Problem

There is an underrepresentation of Women in STEM undergraduate degrees, graduate degrees, and careers.

Our Method

Analyze UW data to identify factors that lead to women student success and identify strategies to increase enrolment, academic success, and careers growth in STEM.

Our Findings

After cleaning, visualizing, and analyzing over 100,000 unique student records containing academic and co-op information, we were able to use machine learning techniques to group students into 6 main clusters with distinct attributes, preferences, and motivations.

These clusters gave the University insights on which groups of female students are strong candidates to pursue successful careers in STEM, but may not be doing so, due to systemic and psychological barriers. This will enable the University of Waterloo to begin developing targeted programs to support and encourage more female students to pursue opportunities in STEM.
