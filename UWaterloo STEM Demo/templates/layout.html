About This Project

The Facts

Accenture's Social Innovation

Accenture’s Social Innovation team is committed to solving real-world I&D issues with a data-driven approach. In this initiative, Accenture partnered with a leading Canadian university to develop an unsupervised clustering model aimed at identifying the qualitative and quantitative attributes that lead to student success.   The resulting findings and associated student personas allowed the University to take an innovative approach at attracting, empowering and supporting their students, by create more targeted student initiatives. 

Data Exploration

We analyzed a dataset of over 20,000 students looking at data points around demographics, academics, admissions, registration, and co-op records.

Unsupervised Machine Learning

After building the unsupervised model and running 96 iterations of the clustering algorithm, we identified 6 distinct groups and built descriptive profiles for each. These profiles helped us to understand prominent success factors and the strength of correlations between factors.

Human Centric Recommendations

We identified insights on how to better attract, empower, and support women in STEM through both academics and co-ops. These insights have also been leveraged to enhance current academic and experiential learning programs, and further promote student success.
